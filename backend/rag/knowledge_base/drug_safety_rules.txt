DRUG SAFETY RULES AND CONTRAINDICATIONS - AYUSH MEDICINE SAFETY GUIDELINES

PREGNANCY SAFETY RULES - GARBHINI PARIHARA

Category 1: Absolutely Contraindicated Herbs in Pregnancy
These herbs should never be used during pregnancy due to high risk of miscarriage, birth defects, or other complications:

- Vacha (Acorus calamus) - Can cause uterine contractions and miscarriage
- Jatamansi (Nardostachys jatamansi) - May cause uterine stimulation
- Kustha (Saussurea costus) - Contraindicated in all trimesters
- Haridra (Curcuma longa) in high doses - Can stimulate uterine contractions
- Daruharidra (Berberis aristata) - Contains berberine, contraindicated in pregnancy
- Guggulu (Commiphora mukul) - Can cause uterine bleeding
- Eranda (Ricinus communis) - Strong purgative, can induce abortion
- Karanja (Pongamia pinnata) - Toxic to fetus
- Bhallataka (Semecarpus anacardium) - Highly toxic during pregnancy
- Kuchla (Strychnos nux-vomica) - Extremely toxic, contraindicated

Category 2: Use with Extreme Caution in Pregnancy
These herbs may be used only under strict medical supervision with dosage adjustment:

- Ashwagandha (Withania somnifera) - Use only in second trimester under supervision
- Shatavari (Asparagus racemosus) - Generally safe but monitor for uterine effects
- Guduchi (Tinospora cordifolia) - Safe in moderate doses
- Amalaki (Emblica officinalis) - Generally safe in food amounts
- Haritaki (Terminalia chebula) - Avoid in first trimester
- Bibhitaka (Terminalia bellirica) - Use with caution
- Pippali (Piper longum) - Mild uterine stimulant, use cautiously
- Sunthi (Zingiber officinale) - Safe in culinary amounts
- Maricha (Piper nigrum) - Use in moderation
- Tulsi (Ocimum sanctum) - Generally safe in moderate doses

Pregnancy-Specific Formulation Rules:
- Avoid all bhasma preparations during pregnancy
- Mineral preparations should be avoided unless absolutely necessary
- Kshara preparations are contraindicated
- All purificatory therapies (Panchakarma) are contraindicated
- Emesis and purgation therapies are absolutely contraindicated
- Bloodletting procedures are contraindicated
- Enema therapy should be avoided unless medically necessary

Dosage Adjustments During Pregnancy:
- Reduce standard adult dosage by 50-75%
- Use only the minimal effective dose
- Limit treatment duration to shortest necessary period
- Monitor for any adverse effects continuously
- Discontinue immediately if any adverse effects occur

CARDIAC SAFETY RULES - HRIDROGA CHIKITSA

Category 1: Cardiac Stimulants - Use with Caution
These herbs can affect heart rate, rhythm, or contractility:

- Vacha (Acorus calamus) - Can cause tachycardia and arrhythmias
- Jatamansi (Nardostachys jatamansi) - May affect heart rhythm
- Kustha (Saussurea costus) - Can affect cardiac function
- Pippali (Piper longum) - Stimulant, may increase heart rate
- Maricha (Piper nigrum) - Cardiac stimulant
- Bhallataka (Semecarpus anacardium) - Can affect cardiovascular system
- Kuchla (Strychnos nux-vomica) - Can cause cardiac arrhythmias
- Eranda (Ricinus communis) - Can affect cardiac function
- Karanja (Pongamia pinnata) - May affect heart rhythm
- Guggulu (Commiphora mukul) - Can affect lipid profile and cardiac function

Category 2: Cardiac-Safe Herbs
These herbs are generally safe for cardiac patients:

- Arjuna (Terminalia arjuna) - Cardioprotective, actually beneficial
- Guduchi (Tinospora cordifolia) - Cardioprotective antioxidant
- Amalaki (Emblica officinalis) - Antioxidant, cardioprotective
- Haritaki (Terminalia chebula) - Mild cardiac effects, generally safe
- Bibhitaka (Terminalia bellirica) - Generally safe for heart patients
- Ashwagandha (Withania somnifera) - Adaptogenic, generally safe
- Shatavari (Asparagus racemosus) - Generally safe
- Tulsi (Ocimum sanctum) - Mild cardiac effects, generally safe
- Sunthi (Zingiber officinale) - Generally safe in normal doses
- Mandukaparni (Centella asiatica) - Generally safe

Cardiac Patient Guidelines:
- Monitor blood pressure and heart rate regularly
- Avoid herbs that stimulate cardiovascular system
- Use cardioprotective herbs under medical supervision
- Adjust doses based on cardiac condition severity
- Avoid combination with cardiac medications without consultation
- Monitor for drug-herb interactions
- Discontinue if any cardiac symptoms worsen

PEDIATRIC SAFETY RULES - BALA ROGA CHIKITSA

Age-Specific Dosage Guidelines:
- Infants (0-1 year): 1/8 to 1/4 adult dose
- Children (1-5 years): 1/4 to 1/3 adult dose
- Children (5-10 years): 1/3 to 1/2 adult dose
- Children (10-16 years): 1/2 to 3/4 adult dose
- Adolescents (16+ years): Full adult dose with caution

Contraindicated Herbs in Children Under 5:
- Vacha (Acorus calamus) - Neurotoxic in young children
- Jatamansi (Nardostachys jatamansi) - Too strong for young children
- Kustha (Saussurea costus) - Can cause digestive upset
- Bhallataka (Semecarpus anacardium) - Highly toxic
- Kuchla (Strychnos nux-vomica) - Extremely toxic
- Guggulu (Commiphora mukul) - Too strong for young children
- Eranda (Ricinus communis) - Strong purgative
- Karanja (Pongamia pinnata) - Toxic to children
- Haritaki (Terminalia chebula) - Can cause digestive upset
- Bibhitaka (Terminalia bellirica) - Can be too strong

Safe Herbs for Children:
- Amalaki (Emblica officinalis) - Rich in vitamin C, safe
- Guduchi (Tinospora cordifolia) - Immunomodulator, safe
- Tulsi (Ocimum sanctum) - Mild, generally safe
- Sunthi (Zingiber officinale) - Safe in small doses
- Pippali (Piper longum) - Use in very small doses
- Haritaki (Terminalia chebula) - Use with caution in children over 5
- Ashwagandha (Withania somnifera) - Use only for children over 10
- Shatavari (Asparagus racemosus) - Generally safe
- Mandukaparni (Centella asiatica) - Brain tonic, safe
- Yashtimadhu (Glycyrrhiza glabra) - Safe in moderate doses

Pediatric Formulation Rules:
- Use sweetening agents like honey or jaggery
- Avoid bitter formulations for young children
- Use liquid formulations when possible
- Avoid strong purgatives and emetics
- Monitor for allergic reactions carefully
- Start with very small doses and increase gradually
- Use palatable preparations to ensure compliance

ELDERLY SAFETY RULES - JARA CHIKITSA

Age-Related Considerations:
- Reduced digestive capacity - use easily digestible preparations
- Impaired kidney function - monitor renal parameters
- Cardiovascular fragility - avoid cardiac stimulants
- Neurological sensitivity - avoid neurotoxic herbs
- Immune system changes - adjust immunomodulators
- Metabolic slowdown - reduce dosage accordingly

Contraindicated in Elderly Patients:
- Vacha (Acorus calamus) - Neurotoxic in elderly
- Jatamansi (Nardostachys jatamansi) - Can cause confusion
- Kuchla (Strychnos nux-vomica) - Extremely toxic
- Bhallataka (Semecarpus anacardium) - Too strong for elderly
- Guggulu (Commiphora mukul) - Can affect cardiovascular system
- Eranda (Ricinus communis) - Strong purgative
- Karanja (Pongamia pinnata) - Toxic to elderly
- Haritaki (Terminalia chebula) - Can cause dehydration
- Bibhitaka (Terminalia bellirica) - Can cause digestive upset
- Kustha (Saussurea costus) - Can affect elderly physiology

Safe Herbs for Elderly:
- Ashwagandha (Withania somnifera) - Adaptogenic, rejuvenating
- Guduchi (Tinospora cordifolia) - Immunomodulator, safe
- Amalaki (Emblica officinalis) - Antioxidant, safe
- Shatavari (Asparagus racemosus) - Nourishing, safe
- Tulsi (Ocimum sanctum) - Mild, generally safe
- Sunthi (Zingiber officinale) - Safe in normal doses
- Arjuna (Terminalia arjuna) - Cardioprotective
- Mandukaparni (Centella asiatica) - Neuroprotective
- Yashtimadhu (Glycyrrhiza glabra) - Use with caution
- Pippali (Piper longum) - Use in reduced doses

Elderly-Specific Guidelines:
- Start with 1/4 to 1/2 adult dose
- Increase very gradually if needed
- Monitor for side effects more frequently
- Avoid combination with multiple medications
- Consider drug-herb interactions carefully
- Use single herbs rather than complex formulations
- Monitor kidney and liver function regularly

RENAL SAFETY RULES - MUTRAVHA SROTAS DISEASES

Nephrotoxic Herbs - Avoid in Renal Patients:
- Vacha (Acorus calamus) - Can cause renal damage
- Jatamansi (Nardostachys jatamansi) - May affect kidney function
- Bhallataka (Semecarpus anacardium) - Nephrotoxic
- Kuchla (Strychnos nux-vomica) - Can affect kidneys
- Guggulu (Commiphora mukul) - May affect renal function
- Eranda (Ricinus communis) - Can cause renal irritation
- Karanja (Pongamia pinnata) - Nephrotoxic
- Haritaki (Terminalia chebula) - Use with caution
- Bibhitaka (Terminalia bellirica) - Monitor renal function
- Kustha (Saussurea costus) - Can affect kidneys

Renal-Safe Herbs:
- Guduchi (Tinospora cordifolia) - Renoprotective
- Amalaki (Emblica officinalis) - Antioxidant, safe
- Punarnava (Boerhavia diffusa) - Diuretic, renoprotective
- Varuna (Crataeva religiosa) - Renoprotective
- Gokshura (Tribulus terrestris) - Mild diuretic, safe
- Palash (Butea monosperma) - Renoprotective
- Shigru (Moringa oleifera) - Nutritive, safe
- Kokilaksha (Asteracantha longifolia) - Renoprotective
- Kulattha (Dolichos biflorus) - Generally safe
- Yava (Hordeum vulgare) - Diuretic, safe

Renal Patient Guidelines:
- Monitor creatinine and BUN regularly
- Avoid nephrotoxic herbs completely
- Use renoprotective herbs under supervision
- Adjust doses based on renal function
- Avoid diuretic herbs in severe renal failure
- Monitor electrolyte balance
- Discontinue if renal function deteriorates

- Vijaysar (Pterocarpus marsupium) - Hypoglycemic

ALLERGY AND SENSITIVITY RULES

Common Allergenic Herbs:
- Bhallataka (Semecarpus anacardium) - Contact dermatitis
- Guggulu (Commiphora mukul) - Allergic reactions
- Eranda (Ricinus communis) - Allergic reactions
- Karanja (Pongamia pinnata) - Allergic reactions
- Kustha (Saussurea costus) - May cause allergies
- Vacha (Acorus calamus) - Allergic potential
- Jatamansi (Nardostachys jatamansi) - May cause allergies

Allergy Testing Guidelines:
- Perform patch test for topical preparations
- Start with very small oral doses
- Monitor for allergic reactions
- Have emergency medications available
- Discontinue immediately if reaction occurs
- Document allergic reactions for future reference

DRUG INTERACTION RULES

With Anticoagulants:
- Guggulu (Commiphora mukul) - May enhance anticoagulation
- Garlic (Allium sativum) - May enhance anticoagulation
- Ginkgo (Ginkgo biloba) - May enhance anticoagulation
- Ashwagandha (Withania somnifera) - May affect coagulation

With Antihypertensives:
- Rauwolfia (Rauwolfia serpentina) - Additive effects
- Ashwagandha (Withania somnifera) - Additive effects
- Arjuna (Terminalia arjuna) - May enhance effects

With Antidiabetics:
- Gudmar (Gymnema sylvestre) - Additive hypoglycemic
- Karela (Momordica charantia) - Additive hypoglycemic
- Jambu (Syzygium cumini) - Additive hypoglycemic

With CNS Drugs:
- Vacha (Acorus calamus) - May interact with psychotropics
- Jatamansi (Nardostachys jatamansi) - May interact with CNS drugs
- Ashwagandha (Withania somnifera) - May interact with CNS drugs

GENERAL SAFETY PRINCIPLES

1. Start Low, Go Slow: Always start with low doses and increase gradually
2. Monitor Continuously: Watch for adverse effects continuously
3. Know Contraindications: Be aware of all contraindications
4. Check Interactions: Verify no drug-herb interactions
5. Use Quality Herbs: Ensure herb quality and authenticity
6. Proper Preparation: Use correct preparation methods
7. Appropriate Duration: Limit treatment duration as needed
8. Professional Supervision: Use under professional guidance
9. Emergency Preparedness: Have emergency measures ready
10. Documentation: Document all treatments and outcomes
