NAMASTE CODE DESCRIPTIONS - AYUSH TERMINOLOGY SYSTEM

AYU-D-0001: Vataja Jwara
Vata-type fever is characterized by dryness in the body, variable temperature patterns, and associated symptoms such as body pain, anxiety, and insomnia. The fever tends to fluctuate throughout the day, often worsening in the late afternoon and evening. Patients typically experience dry cough, cracking joints, and a feeling of coldness despite having elevated temperature. The condition is aggravated by dry foods, excessive travel, and irregular eating habits. Treatment focuses on pacifying Vata dosha through warm, oily foods and gentle therapies.

AYU-D-0002: Pittaja Jwara
Pitta-type fever presents with high temperature, burning sensation throughout the body, excessive thirst, and sweating. The fever is typically steady and high, with patients feeling hot to touch. Associated symptoms include yellowish discoloration of skin and eyes, bitter taste in mouth, and irritability. The condition worsens with spicy foods, sun exposure, and anger. Treatment involves cooling therapies and bitter-tasting herbs to pacify Pitta dosha.

AYU-D-0003: Kaphaja Jwara
Kapha-type fever is characterized by heaviness in the body, congestion, and low-grade temperature that persists for extended periods. Patients experience excessive salivation, sweet taste in mouth, and lethargy. The fever is often accompanied by respiratory symptoms such as cough with expectoration and nasal congestion. The condition is aggravated by heavy, sweet foods and cold, humid weather. Treatment focuses on drying and lightening therapies to reduce Kapha.

AYU-D-0101: Udakameha
Udakameha represents diabetes mellitus with polyuria where urine is tasteless and abundant. Patients experience frequent urination, especially at night, excessive thirst, and dryness of mouth. The condition is associated with debility, weight loss, and general weakness. It's considered a Kapha disorder affecting the kidneys and urinary system. Treatment includes diuretic herbs, dietary restrictions, and lifestyle modifications to reduce Kapha dosha.

AYU-D-0102: Ikshuvalikarasa
This form of diabetes is specifically caused by excessive consumption of sugarcane and sweet substances. Patients develop sweet cravings, weight gain, and fatigue. The urine becomes sweet and patients may experience skin infections and delayed wound healing. It's primarily a Kapha disorder resulting from dietary indiscretions. Treatment emphasizes strict dietary control and herbs that improve metabolism.

AYU-D-0110: Madhumeha
Madhumeha is the most severe form of diabetes where urine resembles honey in color, taste, and consistency. It represents advanced diabetes with complications. Patients experience severe thirst, excessive urination, emaciation, and weakness. The condition affects multiple body systems and can lead to serious complications. It's considered difficult to treat and requires comprehensive management.

AYU-D-0201: Hridroga
Hridroga encompasses all heart diseases in Ayurvedic terminology. It includes conditions affecting the heart and cardiovascular system. Patients may experience chest pain, palpitations, breathlessness, and edema. The condition can be caused by all three doshas but Vata is often the primary factor. Treatment varies based on the predominant dosha and specific manifestations.

AYU-D-0202: Vataja Hridroga
Vata-type heart disease is characterized by irregular heartbeat, anxiety, insomnia, and chest pain that is sharp and migrating. Patients often experience palpitations, especially during stress or at night. The condition is associated with excessive mental work, anxiety, and irregular lifestyle. Treatment focuses on calming Vata through oil therapies and stress management.

AYU-D-0301: Amavata
Amavata is rheumatoid arthritis where Ama (metabolic toxins) combine with Vata to affect joints. Patients experience severe joint stiffness, especially in the morning, swelling, and pain that migrates between joints. The condition is characterized by loss of appetite, indigestion, and general debility. It's considered a serious autoimmune condition requiring long-term management.

AYU-D-0302: Sandhivata
Sandhivata represents osteoarthritis and degenerative joint diseases. Patients experience joint pain that worsens with movement, reduced mobility, and crepitus. The condition primarily affects weight-bearing joints and is associated with aging and wear and tear. Treatment focuses on reducing Vata and nourishing joint tissues.

AYU-D-0401: Kamala
Kamala is jaundice characterized by yellow discoloration of skin, eyes, and mucous membranes. Patients experience loss of appetite, weakness, fever, and dark-colored urine. The condition is primarily a Pitta disorder affecting the liver and digestive system. Treatment involves liver-purifying herbs and dietary restrictions.

AYU-D-0402: Rakta Pitta
Rakta Pitta encompasses blood disorders with bleeding tendencies. Patients experience easy bruising, bleeding from gums, nosebleeds, and heavy menstrual bleeding. The condition is caused by aggravated Pitta affecting blood tissue. Treatment focuses on cooling therapies and blood-purifying herbs.

AYU-D-0501: Pratishyaya
Pratishyaya is chronic rhinitis with persistent nasal congestion and discharge. Patients experience headache, post-nasal drip, and reduced sense of smell. The condition is primarily Kapha disorder affecting the upper respiratory tract. Treatment includes nasal cleansing and herbs that reduce Kapha.

AYU-D-0502: Tamaka Shvasa
Tamaka Shvasa is bronchial asthma characterized by wheezing, shortness of breath, and difficulty breathing especially at night. Patients experience chest tightness, cough with expectoration, and anxiety during attacks. The condition is primarily Vata disorder affecting the respiratory system. Treatment focuses on bronchodilator herbs and avoiding triggers.

AYU-D-0601: Atisara
Atisara is gastroenteritis with diarrhea, abdominal pain, and dehydration. Patients experience frequent loose stools, abdominal cramps, thirst, and weakness. The condition can be caused by contaminated food, poor hygiene, or digestive impairment. Treatment focuses on rehydration and antimicrobial herbs.

AYU-D-0602: Aruchi
Aruchi is loss of appetite and indigestion. Patients experience bloating, gas, abdominal discomfort, and aversion to food. The condition is primarily Vata disorder affecting digestive fire. Treatment focuses on stimulating appetite and improving digestion.

AYU-D-0701: Mukhapaka
Mukhapaka represents oral ulcers and mouth sores. Patients experience painful ulcers in mouth, burning sensation, and difficulty eating. The condition is primarily Pitta disorder affecting oral mucosa. Treatment includes cooling herbs and oral hygiene.

AYU-D-0702: Danta Rakta
Danta Rakta is bleeding gums and periodontal disease. Patients experience bleeding during brushing, bad breath, and swollen gums. The condition is caused by poor oral hygiene and aggravated Pitta. Treatment focuses on oral care and blood-purifying herbs.

AYU-D-0801: Timira
Timira represents myopia and refractive errors. Patients experience blurred vision for distant objects, headaches, and eye strain. The condition is primarily Vata disorder affecting visual apparatus. Treatment includes eye exercises and nourishing therapies.

AYU-D-0802: Shukra Nasha
Shukra Nasha encompasses sexual dysfunction and infertility. Patients experience low libido, erectile dysfunction, and reduced fertility. The condition is primarily Vata disorder affecting reproductive tissues. Treatment focuses on rejuvenation therapies and stress management.

SID-D-0001: Azhal Noi
Azhal Noi represents heat-related disorders in Siddha medicine. Patients experience fever, burning sensation, thirst, and exhaustion. The condition is caused by excessive heat in the body affecting various systems. Treatment focuses on cooling therapies and heat-reducing herbs.

SID-D-0002: Iyya Noi
Iyya Noi represents wind-related disorders in Siddha medicine. Patients experience gas, bloating, abdominal distension, and irregular bowel movements. The condition is caused by aggravated Vata affecting digestive system. Treatment focuses on gas-relieving herbs and digestive normalization.

SID-D-0003: Vali Noi
Vali Noi represents bile-related disorders in Siddha medicine. Patients experience nausea, vomiting, indigestion, and acid reflux. The condition is caused by aggravated Pitta affecting digestive system. Treatment focuses on anti-emetic herbs and digestive normalization.

UNA-D-0001: Humma
Humma is fever in Unani medicine characterized by elevated body temperature, headache, and body pain. Patients experience loss of appetite, weakness, and sometimes delirium in severe cases. The condition is caused by various factors including infections and environmental changes. Treatment focuses on fever-reducing herbs and supportive care.

UNA-D-0002: Waram
Waram represents inflammation and swelling in Unani medicine. Patients experience localized swelling, redness, pain, and heat in affected area. The condition can affect any part of body and is caused by various factors including injury and infection. Treatment focuses on anti-inflammatory herbs and local applications.

UNA-D-0003: Qabz
Qabz is constipation in Unani medicine. Patients experience difficulty passing stools, hard stools, abdominal discomfort, and bloating. The condition is caused by reduced intestinal motility and dryness. Treatment focuses on laxative herbs and dietary fiber.

UNA-D-0101: Nafas-e-Qillat
Nafas-e-Qillat is breathlessness in Unani medicine. Patients experience shortness of breath, difficulty breathing, and sometimes wheezing. The condition affects respiratory system and can be acute or chronic. Treatment focuses on bronchodilator herbs and respiratory support.

UNA-D-0102: Sual-e-Khun
Sual-e-Khun is bloody cough in Unani medicine. Patients cough up blood, experience chest pain, and sometimes fever. The condition is serious and requires immediate attention. Treatment focuses on hemostatic herbs and treating underlying cause.

UNA-D-0201: Waja-ul-Qalb
Waja-ul-Qalb is cardiac pain in Unani medicine. Patients experience chest pain, palpitations, and anxiety. The condition can be due to various cardiac disorders. Treatment focuses on cardiac tonics and stress management.

UNA-D-0202: Dhagh-e-Qalb
Dhagh-e-Qalb is palpitations in Unani medicine. Patients experience rapid or irregular heartbeat, anxiety, and sometimes chest discomfort. The condition is often stress-related. Treatment focuses on calming herbs and stress reduction.

AYU-D-0901: Unmada
Unmada encompasses mental disorders and psychosis in Ayurveda. Patients experience confusion, agitation, inappropriate behavior, and sometimes violence. The condition affects mental faculties and can be acute or chronic. Treatment focuses on calming herbs and behavioral therapy.

AYU-D-0902: Apasmara
Apasmara represents epilepsy and convulsive disorders. Patients experience seizures, loss of consciousness, confusion, and sometimes injury during seizures. The condition affects nervous system and requires long-term management. Treatment focuses on nervine tonics and seizure control.

AYU-D-1001: Kshata
Kshata represents trauma and injuries in Ayurveda. Patients experience pain, swelling, bleeding, and functional impairment. The condition can affect any part of body and ranges from minor to severe. Treatment focuses on wound healing and pain management.

AYU-D-1002: Bhagna
Bhagna represents fractures and bone injuries. Patients experience severe pain, deformity, immobility, and swelling. The condition affects bones and joints. Treatment focuses on bone setting and healing promotion.

SID-D-0301: Kuttam

SID-D-0302: Malai

UNA-D-0302: Bahas
