ICD-11 TM2 CHAPTER 26 - TRADITIONAL MEDICINE MODULE 2

CHAPTER 26: TRADITIONAL MEDICINE CONDITIONS - CHAPTER 26 TM2

OVERVIEW:
Chapter 26 of ICD-11 represents the Traditional Medicine Module 2 (TM2), which provides a standardized classification system for traditional medicine conditions. This module enables integration of traditional medicine concepts with conventional medical terminology through a structured coding system. TM2 focuses on traditional medicine diagnoses, patterns, and syndromes while maintaining compatibility with the main ICD-11 classification.

STRUCTURE AND ORGANIZATION:
The TM2 chapter is organized into sections based on traditional medicine systems and clinical presentations. Each code represents a specific traditional medicine diagnosis, pattern, or syndrome with detailed descriptions and clinical criteria. The coding system follows a hierarchical structure allowing for varying levels of specificity in diagnosis.

SECTION TM2-SC00: TRADITIONAL MEDICINE - GENERAL CONCEPTS

TM2-SC00: Traditional Medicine Conditions, Unspecified
This code represents traditional medicine conditions that cannot be classified more specifically. It serves as a residual category for cases where the traditional medicine diagnosis is unclear or does not fit into defined categories. This code should be used sparingly and only when more specific diagnosis is not possible.

TM2-SC01: RESPIRATORY SYSTEM DISORDERS IN TRADITIONAL MEDICINE
This section covers respiratory conditions as understood and classified in traditional medicine systems. These include various patterns of respiratory illness based on traditional medicine theories of etiology and pathogenesis.

TM2-SC01.0: Wind-Cold Pattern of Respiratory System
Characterized by acute onset with chills, fever, headache, body aches, nasal congestion with clear discharge, and cough with white sputum. The condition is caused by external wind-cold pathogen invading the lung meridian. Treatment focuses on dispelling wind and dispersing cold.

TM2-SC01.1: Wind-Heat Pattern of Respiratory System
Characterized by fever, sore throat, cough with yellow sputum, nasal congestion with yellow discharge, and thirst. This condition results from wind-heat pathogen affecting the lung system. Treatment emphasizes clearing heat and dispersing wind.

TM2-SC01.2: Lung Qi Deficiency Pattern
Characterized by chronic cough, shortness of breath, spontaneous sweating, fatigue, and weak voice. This pattern reflects deficient lung qi failing to govern respiration and control pores. Treatment focuses on tonifying lung qi.

TM2-SC01.3: Lung Yin Deficiency Pattern
Characterized by dry cough, dry throat, hoarseness, afternoon fever, night sweats, and thin, rapid pulse. This pattern reflects insufficient lung yin to moisten and cool the lungs. Treatment emphasizes nourishing lung yin.

TM2-SC02.3: Food Retention Pattern

TM2-SC04: FEVER PATTERNS IN TRADITIONAL MEDICINE
This section covers various fever patterns classified according to traditional medicine etiology and manifestations. The patterns reflect different types of fever based on pathogenic factors and body response.

TM2-SC04.0: Wind-Cold Fever Pattern
Characterized by chills predominating over fever, headache, body aches, and absence of thirst. This pattern results from external wind-cold pathogen invading the exterior. Treatment emphasizes dispelling wind and cold.

TM2-SC04.1: Wind-Heat Fever Pattern
Characterized by fever predominating over chills, sore throat, thirst, and rapid pulse. This pattern results from external wind-heat pathogen. Treatment focuses on clearing heat and dispersing wind.

TM2-SC04.2: Damp-Heat Fever Pattern
Characterized by persistent fever, heaviness, thirst without desire to drink, and greasy tongue coating. This pattern reflects damp-heat pathogen. Treatment emphasizes clearing damp-heat.

TM2-SC04.3: Yin Deficiency Fever Pattern
Characterized by afternoon or evening fever, night sweats, dry mouth and throat, and red tongue with little coating. This pattern reflects internal heat from yin deficiency. Treatment focuses on nourishing yin and clearing deficiency heat.

TM2-SC13: TRADITIONAL MEDICINE TREATMENT PRINCIPLES
This section covers treatment principles used in traditional medicine for various conditions.

TM2-SC13.0: Dispelling External Pathogens Principle
Used for acute conditions caused by external pathogens. Treatment aims to expel pathogens from the exterior level before they penetrate deeper.

TM2-SC13.1: Clearing Heat Principle
Used for heat-type conditions with fever, inflammation, and redness. Treatment uses cooling herbs and therapies to reduce pathological heat.

TM2-SC13.2: Warming Interior Principle
Used for cold-type conditions with cold limbs, abdominal pain, and loose stools. Treatment uses warming herbs and therapies to dispel cold.

TM2-SC13.3: Tonifying Deficiency Principle
Used for deficient conditions with fatigue, weakness, and poor function. Treatment uses tonifying herbs and therapies to strengthen deficient aspects.
